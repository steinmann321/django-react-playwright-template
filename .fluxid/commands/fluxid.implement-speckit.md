## Quality & Exit Standards

**CRITICAL**: You MUST achieve fully passing tests and complete implementation. Exhaust all proper approaches: try multiple solutions, refactor, read docs, analyze deeply. Push through difficulty—only stop at absolute walls AFTER trying everything legitimate.

**ANTI-CHEAT TRIGGER**: The moment ANY workaround, test-weakening, or compromise appears—**STOP IMMEDIATELY**. Do not implement. Mark tasks incomplete, write FAIL report. Not negotiable.

**Never acceptable**: Weakening/disabling tests, commenting out validations, skipping features, "temporary" hacks/TODOs, lowering standards, anything you wouldn't defend in review.

**Valid stops** (after exhausting legitimate options): External dependency unavailable, architecture limitation needs user decision, quality compromise is only path.

**Invalid stops** (push through): "Too complex", "tests hard", "many errors", "unclear how".

**If thinking "just this once" or "temporary"—you're cheating. STOP.**

Better honest FAIL than compromised implementation — ALWAYS.
